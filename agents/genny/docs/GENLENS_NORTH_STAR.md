# GenLens North Star

Status: Canonical product direction
Owner: Jonathan James
Adopted: July 20, 2026

## North Star

> Establish GenLens as the intelligence system for the generative AI economy:
> Genny tracks generative AI production, Marti tracks AI-native marketing and
> MarTech, and together they turn verified ecosystem signals into trusted,
> actionable decisions.

## Category and promise

**Category:** Intelligence system for the generative AI economy.

**Promise:** GenLens finds meaningful changes, verifies them against credible
sources, connects them to real workflows and economics, and tells a working
operator what decision the evidence supports.

GenLens is not a generic AI-news digest. It is also not a marketing-automation
platform. The product earns trust by converting noisy ecosystem activity into a
smaller set of decision-ready signals.

## Product architecture

### GenLens: the intelligence system

GenLens owns the shared source, verification, signal, history, and delivery
spine. Over time it should become the queryable record of what changed across
the generative AI economy, why it matters, and what operators did in response.

### Genny: the production-intelligence lens

Genny tracks how generative AI changes the making of commercial creative work:
tools, models, pipelines, cost, time, control, rights, and labor. Her primary
audience is creative technologists and production leaders.

Genny answers:

- What changed in generative AI production?
- How does it enter a real workflow?
- What does it change in cost, time, quality, control, rights, or roles?
- What should a production operator test, adopt, avoid, or watch?

### Marti: the marketing-intelligence lens

Marti tracks how generative AI changes marketing systems and distribution:
agentic workflows, paid media, creative performance, stack displacement,
lifecycle, measurement, commerce, search, sales/marketing convergence, and
customer data.

Marti answers:

- What changed in AI-native marketing and MarTech?
- What does it change in acquisition, conversion, retention, measurement, or
  stack economics?
- Which systems are being added, consolidated, displaced, or shut down?
- What should a marketing operator test, migrate, budget for, or watch?

### Convergence: the premium output

Genny and Marti remain distinct lenses. A unified view is valuable only when a
source-backed production change and a source-backed marketing change share a
verified mechanism, workflow, or economic consequence. Convergence is an
editorial conclusion to prove, not a quota to fill and not a third agent voice.

## Positioning language

Recommended hero:

> Intelligence for the generative AI economy.

Recommended explanation:

> Genny tracks how generative AI changes production. Marti tracks how it changes
> marketing. GenLens verifies the signals and turns them into decisions.

“The MarTech of generative AI” may describe Marti's strategic wedge, but it is
too narrow and execution-oriented to define GenLens as a whole.

## Decision-ready signal standard

A GenLens signal is not complete when it has merely been discovered. It becomes
decision-ready when it records:

1. **Change:** what verifiably happened.
2. **Mechanism:** the model, API, workflow, policy, pricing, or market dynamic.
3. **Use case:** who can apply it and where it enters the workflow.
4. **Impact:** the expected effect on cost, time, quality, control, rights,
   revenue, conversion, retention, measurement, or labor.
5. **Evidence:** an exact credible source link and publication date.
6. **Lens:** Genny, Marti, or verified convergence.
7. **Decision:** test, adopt, avoid, migrate, brief, budget, or watch.
8. **Confidence:** how strongly the evidence supports the interpretation.

## North-star metric

The primary product metric is **Weekly Verified Decision Actions (WVDA)**.

A Verified Decision Action occurs when a user attributes one of these actions to
a source-backed GenLens signal:

- starts a test or proof build;
- adopts or rejects a tool or workflow;
- changes a stack, migration, or purchasing decision;
- changes a production, campaign, or measurement plan;
- briefs a client, team, or stakeholder using the signal;
- deliberately adds the signal to a monitored decision queue.

A page view, email open, or source click is useful engagement data but is not a
Verified Decision Action by itself. Until product instrumentation exists, human
review and explicit operator feedback are the proxy; GenLens must not claim a
WVDA number that it cannot observe.

## Supporting proof metrics

These are targets and promotion gates, not claims of current performance:

| Dimension | Metric | Initial target |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial trust | Accepted cards surviving human review | At least 95% across the latest 20 cards |
| Source authority | Cards resolving to primary or authoritative sources | At least 60% per issue |
| Genny reliability | Linked cards and represented production verticals | At least 12 cards across 5 verticals |
| Marti reliability | Linked cards and represented marketing layers | At least 6 cards across 3 layers for 3 consecutive runs |
| Repeat protection | Exact recent URL-fingerprint repeats | Zero unless explicitly approved |
| Positioning comprehension | People who can accurately explain GenLens after a five-second exposure | At least 80% in a small moderated test |
| Decision density | Published cards producing a Verified Decision Action | Establish a baseline before setting a target |
| Retention | Active readers returning because GenLens catches useful signals they would miss | Establish a cohort baseline before setting a target |
| Convergence quality | Cross-lens conclusions surviving human verification | At least 3 before unified delivery becomes a default option |

## Product milestones

### Milestone 1: trustworthy specialist lenses

- Keep Genny's production gate reliable.
- Promote Marti only after its documented three-run and human-review gates pass.
- Preserve first-party sourcing, recency, dedupe, and repeat protection.

### Milestone 2: structured intelligence memory

- Store signals with stable IDs and the decision-ready fields above.
- Preserve accepted and rejected candidates with machine-readable reasons.
- Make claims traceable to evidence and editorial history.

### Milestone 3: observable decision value

- Let users save a signal to a decision queue and record the resulting action.
- Instrument WVDA without treating opens and clicks as decisions.
- Use explicit feedback to improve source and editorial judgment.

### Milestone 4: verified convergence

- Connect Genny and Marti signals by mechanism, workflow, company, tool, and
  economic consequence.
- Publish convergence only after human verification.
- Turn repeated verified patterns into benchmarks, market maps, and paid
  intelligence products.

## Non-goals

- Becoming an exhaustive general-purpose AI news service.
- Padding weak coverage to simulate breadth or cadence.
- Collapsing Genny and Marti into one generic voice.
- Calling correlation between lenses a causal market insight.
- Processing payments, changing production systems, or launching campaigns
  without explicit human approval.
- Claiming a signal graph, Ask capability, forecast calibration, or decision
  metric before it exists and is verified.
