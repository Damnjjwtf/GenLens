# GenLens Product Strategy: Genny Market Intelligence

Last updated: July 18, 2026

## Core Thesis

GenLens should not be a generic AI creative news digest. Genny should turn source signals into market intelligence for AI-native creative production.

The key product move is:

> Signals are not the product. The arbitrage layer is the product.

Genny reads releases, job posts, tutorials, case studies, funding news, research, and production workflows as evidence of how creative work is changing. Her job is to identify the gap between what a workflow is called today and what it is becoming.

## Adapted From GIGSAW

GIGSAW is career operations for one person. GenLens is market intelligence for a creative-technologist audience.

| GIGSAW Mode | GenLens / Genny Equivalent | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Job search | Source scan | Recent signals across curated feeds and job sources |
| Job evaluation | Signal scoring | Novelty, credibility, workflow impact, vertical relevance |

## Operating Modes

### Signal Brief

The daily or weekly briefing. This mode answers:

- What changed?
- Which vertical does it affect?
- What workflow, cost, time, rights, or tool-stack implication does it have?
- Is it source-backed enough to publish?

Signal Brief must avoid static product pages, generic how-to content, and stale listicles.

### Role Radar

The labor-market lens. This mode answers:

- What new role titles are appearing?
- Which old titles are absorbing AI tools?
- Which tool clusters repeat across job posts?
- Which skills are becoming table stakes?
- Which roles are observed, emerging, or forecast?

Role Radar treats job posts as lagging indicators of workflow change.

### Build This

The practical career/product lens. This mode answers:

- What should a Gen AD build this weekend to prove they can do the emerging work?
- Which tools should the proof build use?
- What should the artifact show: before/after, node graph, cost delta, time delta, repeatability, client-readiness, or compliance?

Every Build This item should be live, linkable, and scoped tightly enough to ship.

### Market Map

The strategic map. This mode answers:

- Which companies are leading each vertical?
- Which tools cluster together?
- Which capabilities are missing?
- Which verticals are getting enough signal to promote from watchlist to active coverage?

### Product Lab

The new-product lens. This mode answers:

- What can GenLens sell or ship because Genny now sees the market?
- Which reports, tools, guides, templates, or benchmarks would users pay for?
- Which products are natural extensions of the source graph, tool graph, and role graph?

## Flywheels

### Source Flywheel

Sources reveal tools. Tools reveal companies. Companies reveal jobs. Jobs reveal new roles. New roles reveal better sources.

### Tool Graph Flywheel

Every signal mentions tools. Genny normalizes those tools, maps them to verticals, and tracks which tools appear together.

Examples:

- ComfyUI + Nuke + LoRA + ShotGrid -> Generative VFX pipeline.
- Figma Weave + KeyShot + Shopify -> AI product visualization and presale pipeline.
- ElevenLabs + HeyGen + Descript -> Synthetic presenter / e-learning workflow.
- Unreal + MetaHuman + Move.ai -> Digital human performance pipeline.

### Role Graph Flywheel

Genny tracks job-title mutation:

- CG Artist -> Generative CG Artist
- Motion Designer -> AI Motion Systems Designer
- VFX TD -> Synthetic Production TD
- Product Photographer -> Presale Visual Pipeline Operator
- Game Designer -> Generative Gameplay Prototyper
- Creative Producer -> AI Workflow Producer

### Proof-Build Flywheel

Every emerging role gets a suggested proof build. Proof builds create better user outcomes and better GenLens content.

### Community Feedback Flywheel

Users paste job posts, weird signals, workflows, and tool discoveries. Genny classifies them, enriches the graph, and improves future briefs.

### Product Discovery Flywheel

Genny sees repeated pain before products exist. Those pain patterns become GenLens products.

## Product Opportunities

### This Week's Emerging Creative AI Roles

A recurring briefing with five roles, evidence, tool stack, salary/location signals, and one proof build per role.

### GenLens Role Maps

Public or paid maps that show how traditional creative roles are mutating into AI-native roles.

### Tool Stack Blueprints

Practical stack guides for roles and workflows:

- AI Filmmaking TD stack.
- Product photography presale stack.
- Digital human dialogue stack.
- Generative game prototype stack.

### Proof-Build Generator

Given a target role, Genny proposes a weekend-scoped portfolio build.

### Hiring Briefs

For companies: explain what role they actually need, what tools matter, and how to evaluate candidates.

### Source Quality Dashboard

Show which sources are producing actual signal versus stale or generic content.

### Role Arbitrage Reports

Paid research reports on new creative AI roles over the next 12-18 months.

## Guardrails

- Do not pretend forecast roles already exist.
- Label every role as observed, emerging, or forecast.
- Do not scrape login-only or private job sources.
- Do not collect personal data.
- Do not auto-apply or send outreach.
- Never publish unsourced claims, salary numbers, or tool capabilities.
- If signal quality is weak, say so and improve sources before publishing.
