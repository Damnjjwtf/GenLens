## Core Insight

Generative AI creative jobs are splitting into three families:

1. **Creative hybrid roles**: artists, filmmakers, designers, game developers, editors, compositors, and 3D artists who now use generative AI as part of normal production.
2. **Pipeline / technical artist roles**: TDs, pipeline engineers, design technologists, and creative technologists who connect creative tools, APIs, models, and production workflows.
3. **Inference / platform / ML roles**: engineers and architects who run models, GPUs, orchestration layers, MLOps, and internal AI platforms.

The market is moving from explicit "AI artist" language toward normal production titles that quietly assume AI fluency: CG Artist, Technical Director, Game Engineer, Design Technologist.

## 18-Month Forecast Roles

These are forecasts, not observed titles.

### AI Shot Pipeline Supervisor

Why it may emerge:

- Studios need someone accountable for AI-generated shot throughput, continuity, and QC.

Likely tools:

- ComfyUI, Runway, fal, Nuke, Resolve, ShotGrid, LoRA tooling.

### Model-to-Asset Technical Artist

Why it may emerge:

- Asset creation will increasingly move between text/image/video/3D/model outputs and DCC cleanup.

Likely tools:

- Blender, Maya, Houdini, ComfyUI, Stable Diffusion, ControlNet, LoRAs, Unreal Engine.

### Creative Inference Producer

Why it may emerge:

- Production teams need cost/time/quality tradeoff management across model vendors and GPU infrastructure.

Likely tools:

- fal, cloud GPU providers, Python dashboards, ComfyUI, model routing, internal production trackers.

### Agentic Tools Designer

Why it may emerge:

- AI creative workflows need new interaction design beyond chat: canvas, timeline, graph, procedural agent controls.

Likely tools:

- Figma, Unreal Slate, web front-end frameworks, design systems, AI workflow tools.

### Generative Rights and Provenance Producer

Why it may emerge:

- Creative teams will need someone to manage likeness, training-data constraints, actor/artist consent, source provenance, and delivery documentation.

Likely tools:

- SAG-AFTRA AI resources, contract systems, metadata/provenance tools, asset managers, studio legal workflows.
